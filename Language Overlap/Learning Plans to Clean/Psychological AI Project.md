Ai project that ingest psychological content from specific youtubers using the text data to train and develop models on it to be a better psychological analyzer agent type thing


Great — here’s a beginner-friendly learning roadmap that blends **generative AI fundamentals** with **psychological insight**. Think of it as “learning how minds (and machines that mimic them) work” in parallel.

---

## 1. Foundations: What Is Generative AI and Why It Matters Psychologically

**Goal:** Understand how models like GPT “think” and how that mimics human cognition.

**Watch:**
- 3Blue1Brown's *What is GPT?* – beautifully visual  
- Intro videos from DeepLearning.ai or Khan Academy on neural networks  

**Read:**
- *How Does ChatGPT Work?* – OpenAI’s official explanation  
- Intro to Transformers: Jay Alammar’s *Illustrated Transformer*  

**Reflect:**
> Notice how it’s all about patterns, memory, and prediction — just like your mind when anxious or excited.

---

## 2. Prompting and Language as Psychology

**Goal:** Learn how the structure of questions, commands, or thoughts affects behavior — human and machine.

**Try:**
- Give ChatGPT emotional prompts like:  
  - “Act as my inner critic”  
  - “Help me challenge a negative thought”  
- Notice how slight phrasing changes shift the output — just like therapy.

**Learn:**
- Prompt engineering basics: how to be clear, structured, and intentional (plenty of free guides)  
- Explore how this maps to CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) — reframing, pattern recognition, etc.

**Reflect:**
> Which patterns in language lead to insight? Which ones loop back to self-criticism?

---

## 3. Bias, Training Data & Social Conditioning

**Goal:** Understand how inputs shape outputs — in AI and in humans.

**Read:**
- *The Alignment Problem* by Brian Christian  
- Articles on AI bias — explore what datasets models are trained on  

**Reflect:**
> What “datasets” shaped your own mind growing up?  
> Where are your thought patterns mimicking external expectations?

---

## 4. Creativity, Identity & Mental Models

**Goal:** Explore how generative systems (including your mind) construct stories, identities, and beliefs.

**Try:**
- Ask ChatGPT to generate different versions of your life story:  
  - Stoic  
  - Romantic  
  - Anxious  
  - Optimistic  
- Use image models (like DALL·E or Midjourney) to create metaphors of your mental state

**Reflect:**
> What narratives feel empowering?  
> Which ones are default but unhelpful?

---

## 5. Build Your Own (Optional but Powerful)

**Goal:** Actually tinker with a simple model to deepen your intuition.

**Tools:**
- Use ChatGPT Custom GPTs  
- Explore Google’s Teachable Machine or Hugging Face for no-code AI  
- Play with Python (Jupyter notebooks + Colab) if you're technically inclined

**Reflect:**
> What happens when you try to “train” a model to mimic you?  
> Where does it misunderstand you — and why?

---

## Bonus Psychological Concepts That Pair Well

- **Predictive processing** – the brain as a prediction machine  
- **Cognitive distortions and mental filters** – e.g., personalization, catastrophizing  
- **Narrative identity theory** – your “self” as a story you’re telling  
- **Self-supervised learning** – how children and AI both learn by absorbing patterns
