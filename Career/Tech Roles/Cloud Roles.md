There are several non-software engineering roles that may require the use of AWS (Amazon Web Services) due to its widespread adoption across industries for various purposes. Some of these roles include:  
  
1. **DevOps Engineer:** While this role may involve software engineering tasks, DevOps engineers primarily focus on streamlining the development and deployment processes through automation, continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD), and infrastructure as code (IaC) practices, often utilizing AWS services for managing infrastructure.  
  
2. **System Administrator:** System administrators are responsible for managing and maintaining IT infrastructure, including servers, networks, and databases. They may use AWS for provisioning, monitoring, and managing virtual servers, storage, and networking resources.  
  
3. **Cloud Architect:** Cloud architects design and implement cloud-based solutions for organizations, including planning, architecture design, migration, and optimization of cloud environments. They leverage AWS services to build scalable, resilient, and cost-effective cloud architectures.  
  
4. **Data Engineer:** Data engineers are responsible for designing, building, and maintaining data pipelines and data infrastructure for collecting, storing, processing, and analyzing data. They may use AWS services such as Amazon S3, Amazon Redshift, AWS Glue, and Amazon EMR for managing big data workloads.  
  
5. **Solution Architect:** Solution architects design end-to-end solutions for addressing business requirements, integrating various technologies and services to meet organizational goals. They leverage AWS services to architect scalable, reliable, and secure solutions for different use cases.  
  
6. **Technical Sales/Pre-Sales Engineer:** Technical sales or pre-sales engineers provide technical expertise and support to sales teams and customers, helping them understand and evaluate cloud solutions. They may use AWS to demonstrate product capabilities, design solutions, and provide technical guidance to customers.  
  
7. **Data Scientist:** Data scientists analyze large datasets to extract insights and make data-driven decisions. They may use AWS for data storage, processing, and analytics, leveraging services like Amazon S3, Amazon Redshift, Amazon Athena, and Amazon SageMaker for machine learning.  
  
8. **IT Operations Manager:** IT operations managers oversee the day-to-day operations of IT infrastructure and services, ensuring availability, performance, and security. They may use AWS for monitoring, troubleshooting, and managing cloud resources to meet service level agreements (SLAs) and operational requirements.  
  
9. **Cybersecurity Analyst:** Cybersecurity analysts monitor and protect IT systems and data from security threats and vulnerabilities. They may use AWS security services such as AWS Identity and Access Management (IAM), AWS Web Application Firewall (WAF), and AWS Security Hub to secure cloud environments and detect/respond to security incidents.  
  
These are just a few examples of roles outside of software engineering that may require familiarity with AWS due to its extensive use in modern IT infrastructure and cloud computing environments. As AWS continues to evolve and expand its services, the demand for professionals with AWS skills across various roles is likely to increase.
