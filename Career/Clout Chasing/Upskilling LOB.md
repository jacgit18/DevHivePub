**Reflecting on My Journey: From Business System Analyst to Software Engineer**  
  
As I reflect on my time as a Business Systems Analyst, much of my work focused on improving internal business processes, particularly around upskilling plans, designing bootcamps, and conducting needs assessments for software engineer development. The goal was always to ensure that upskilling efforts aligned with both individual growth and business outcomes.  
  
Now, working as a software engineer at Capital One, I find myself thinking about my own upskilling journey and what specific knowledge is essential for me to perform at my best. This reflection naturally brings up questions like:  
- How much should engineers know about the specific **line of business (LOB)** they’re supporting?  
- Is it worth focusing upskilling efforts on industry knowledge or project-specific details?  
- To what depth does this knowledge need to go for engineers to excel in their day-to-day work?  
- What type of engineering workflows or roles benefit most from LOB or industry-specific upskilling?  
  
### What I’ve Learned So Far  
The relationship between **LOB knowledge** and **engineering roles** varies:  
- For **cloud engineers**, much of the work revolves around infrastructure, deployment, and scaling, which can often be abstracted from LOB details. However, roles that intersect with **AI/ML models**, **data warehousing**, or **compliance** often demand deeper LOB knowledge.  
- For **DevOps engineers**, understanding compliance, SLAs, or industry-specific monitoring metrics becomes critical when configuring pipelines or collaborating with development teams.  
  
Contrast this with **feature development or testing**, where understanding the specific business logic and user needs is non-negotiable—building an e-commerce checkout system, for example, requires knowledge of payment processes, tax rules, and user behavior.  
  
### A Personal Take on Upskilling  
Thinking about my own role, I’ve started examining what business knowledge I need to refine. For instance:  
- **What’s the cost-benefit of upskilling around LOB knowledge for my projects?**  
- **How do I balance technical expertise with industry insights to create maximum impact?**  
- **What workflows or tools can help streamline the learning process for engineers in similar roles?**  
  
### Broader Implications  
Reflecting on my experience as both a Business Systems Analyst and a Software Engineer, I’ve realized that upskilling strategies must be tailored to the role, the industry, and the workflows involved. Whether designing internal bootcamps or improving my own skills, the key is aligning efforts with the unique demands of the role and the value they bring to the business.
