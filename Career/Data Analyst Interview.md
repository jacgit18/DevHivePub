In a data analyst interview, the rules regarding the use of external data during a case study typically depend on the specific guidelines provided by the interviewing company. Here are some general possibilities:  
  
1. **Strictly Provided Data**: In many cases, you are expected to work exclusively with the dataset provided by the company. This allows the interviewers to evaluate your ability to analyze and derive insights from a specific set of data under controlled conditions.  
  
2. **Supplementary Data Allowed**: Some companies might allow or even encourage you to bring in additional data if it can enhance your analysis. If this is the case, it's usually specified in the instructions. Bringing supplementary data can show initiative and resourcefulness, but it's essential to ensure the additional data is relevant and credible.  
  
3. **Clarify Ambiguities**: If the instructions are unclear about using external data, it's a good idea to ask the interviewer for clarification. This shows that you are attentive to guidelines and willing to ensure you are following the rules correctly.  
  
4. **Assumptions and Justifications**: If you decide to bring in external data without explicit instructions, make sure to clearly state your assumptions and justify why the additional data is relevant and how it enhances your analysis.  
  
5. **Focus on the Given Task**: Even if external data is allowed, ensure that your primary focus remains on the task and data provided by the company. Supplementary data should support, not overshadow, your main analysis.  
  
Ultimately, the key is to follow the specific guidelines provided by the interviewers and to communicate effectively about your data sources and the rationale behind using any supplementary data.
