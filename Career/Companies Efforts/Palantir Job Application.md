### Backend Service Design and Implementation Experience  

At TracFlo, I designed and implemented over 13 backend features to support complex workflows in construction project management. These included ticket creation and editing, as well as functionalities for managing material and equipment orders. These features supported an average of 135+ tickets per company project, accommodating 50+ general contractors and subcontracting companies, with over 35 active users.  

If revisiting this project, I would begin with a comprehensive needs assessment, addressing both functional and non-functional requirements while focusing on user goals. Key considerations would include:  
- **Role-Based Access**: Ensuring tailored permissions for different user types.  
- **Data Handling**: Incorporating features like data export, system logs, and regulatory reporting.  
- **Performance, Security, and Scalability**: Prioritizing these non-functional aspects for a robust and future-proof solution.  

To achieve this, I would:  
- Leverage **open-source technologies** with strong community support for maintainability.  
- Utilize **cloud infrastructure** for scalability and resource efficiency.  
- Optimize data storage and processing to handle growing user needs.  

Lastly, I would ensure compliance with relevant regulations and evaluate the cost-effectiveness of the solution to support scalability and long-term growth.

### Favorite Project: DevHiveMind  

My favorite project is **DevHiveMind**, a personal initiative I started while working at TD Bank. Initially, it was a way to organize my technical notes as I delved deeper into data and engineering practices. During that time, I participated in various bootcamps and upskilling programs, which allowed me to focus not only on coding but also on understanding broader engineering concepts.

What makes DevHiveMind so meaningful to me is how it evolved from a simple note collection into a centralized, comprehensive **software development encyclopedia** built within Obsidian. It now serves as a valuable resource for developers of all levels, covering a wide range of topics, best practices, and in-depth explanations of tools and technologies.  

A critical factor in its success was the creation of **detailed metadata** for each note. By developing a robust tagging and classification system, I linked related topics seamlessly, making it easier to search and retrieve specific insights. This added layer of organization transformed DevHiveMind into a dynamic and interactive resource that grows alongside my knowledge.

What I love most about this project is its **practicality** and adaptability. It reflects both my technical skills and my passion for learning, teaching, and sharing knowledge with others. DevHiveMind is not just a tool for myself but a growing repository that embodies the joy of continuous learning and collaboration.
