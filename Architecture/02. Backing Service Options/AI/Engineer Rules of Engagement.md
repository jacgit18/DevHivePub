## 📜 Rules of Engagement – Asking for Help as a Software Engineer

---

### **1. Pre-Request Checklist – Don’t Cry Wolf**

Before you interrupt someone, confirm you’ve done your due diligence:

- ✅ **Rubber-ducked** the issue (spoken or written it out).
    
- ✅ Checked **logs, stack traces, and errors**.
    
- ✅ **Searched the codebase** (VSCode, grep, or global search).
    
- ✅ Reviewed **internal docs, wikis, and past tickets**.
    
- ✅ Tried relevant solutions **and documented failures**.
    

**Rule of Thumb:**  
If you’ve spent **30–60 focused minutes** and aren’t closer to a solution—or can’t even frame the right question—stop. You’re now burning time, not building independence.

---

### **2. When to Ask – The “Time-to-Unblock” Rule**

- 🟢 **Low priority** – Ask within a day or two; mark as “no rush.”
    
- 🟡 **Medium priority** – Ask after **30–60 minutes** of effort.
    
- 🔴 **Blocking/urgent** – Ask **immediately** with context. No apologies.
    

---

### **3. How to Ask – The “Context Sandwich”**

**Format:**  
**TL;DR:** I’m trying to do **X**, but **Y** is happening. **Z** didn’t work. Can you take a quick look?

- **Goal:** 1-line objective.
    
- **What I’ve tried:** Up to 3 bullets.
    
- **Roadblock/Error:** Short message or behavior.
    
- **Suspected cause** _(optional)_: Your best guess.
    

**Why it works:**

- Shows effort.
    
- Saves their time.
    
- Gets you better answers faster.
    

---

### **4. Frequency Guidelines – The “2–3 Touchpoints” Rule**

- 🧠 **1–2** well-prepped asks/week is healthy.
    
- 😬 **4+ direct asks without context** = looks needy or disorganized.
    
- 🫱 Consider **scheduled coworking** for thorny problems.
    
- **Pro Tip:** Bundle non-urgent issues into a weekly “debug hour.”
    

---

### **5. Live Debugging Etiquette**

When pairing:

- State your goal: _“Let’s try to reproduce this”_ or _“Sanity check my mental model.”_
    
- Narrate thinking, not just clicks.
    
- Ask when you don’t understand—don’t fake comprehension.
    
- Take notes. Send a follow-up if needed.
    

---

### **6. Anti-Patterns to Avoid**

|Anti-Pattern|Why It’s a Problem|Do This Instead|
|---|---|---|
|“Hey, quick question…” with no context|Derails them mid-task|Drop the full question in first message|
|Asking repeatedly without trying|Wears down trust|Show your attempt log|
|Going silent when stuck|Bottlenecks delivery|Ask for a small unblock|
|Waiting too long to ask|Burns time, blocks others|Follow “time-to-unblock” rule|
|Pairing without engaging|Signals passivity|Verbally debug your thoughts|

---

### **7. Skill to Build – Collaborative Debugging**

- Frame your confusion clearly.
    
- Have a mental model before pairing; update it live.
    
- Ask: _“Can you reframe that?”_ when unclear.
    
- Study their debugging moves: _“How did you know to check there?”_
