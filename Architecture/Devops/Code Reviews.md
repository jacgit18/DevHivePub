### 1. **Peer-Based Reviews**
   - Code reviews typically involve team members at a similar level or anyone actively working on the same codebase. In most cases, this collaborative approach allows multiple team members to review each other’s code, fostering a sense of collective ownership and responsibility.
   - The primary goals of peer-based reviews are to uphold code quality, catch potential errors, and maintain stylistic and functional consistency across the codebase. This approach ensures that all engineers, regardless of their position, can provide input, making it a key tool for both knowledge sharing and team alignment.

### 2. **Guidance from Senior Engineers**
   - In many teams, senior engineers or tech leads will review code for critical features or architectural decisions, adding a layer of technical insight and strategic oversight. While this can introduce an element of hierarchy, it’s typically complementary rather than exclusive. Senior engineers often participate as reviewers alongside peers, with their feedback serving as additional guidance rather than a sole approval step.
   - Their insights can be particularly valuable for complex changes or when decisions impact broader architectural patterns. While their feedback may carry more weight, it’s part of a collaborative effort to maintain code quality across levels.

### 3. **Team-Specific Practices**
   - Many teams blend peer and senior reviews based on the nature and complexity of the changes. For smaller, straightforward pull requests (PRs), peer reviews from engineers familiar with the codebase are typically sufficient.
   - However, larger, more intricate changes may require review or final approval from a senior developer, especially for code with long-term impact. This mix is often governed by team norms, the project’s scale, and each developer’s familiarity with the codebase.
  
In sum, code reviews are rooted in a collaborative peer review structure, designed more to enhance code quality, share knowledge, and foster inclusivity rather than to reinforce hierarchy. This approach helps create a balanced environment where contributions from all team members are valued, promoting growth and consistency across the team.
