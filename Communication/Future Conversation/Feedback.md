### Perspective and Empathy in Feedback  
When engaging with others, particularly during moments of giving and receiving feedback, it's crucial to step into their perspective. Ask yourself: _What does this moment feel like for them?_ Understanding their emotions and viewpoint can help you gauge where to draw the line between constructive criticism and harmful feedback. Effective feedback requires balance—avoiding over-criticism that might break someone down while ensuring they feel supported and capable of growth. The goal is to leave them standing stronger, not diminished, after the interaction.

### The Balance Between Building and Cutting  
Human interactions often reflect a duality of building and cutting. Society sometimes frames men as builders—constructing the physical world and shaping it through their actions or words—while women are seen as cutters, trimming hair, nails, or cutting with their words. But in truth, everyone uses these tools of creation and refinement; it’s the intent and application that matter.

You can build something destructive, such as negative behaviors, toxic relationships, or unhealthy environments—whether in your children, colleagues, or even yourself. Conversely, cutting can be a powerful act of improvement: removing distractions, shedding illusions, and clearing the way for truth. Both building and cutting can serve as tools for growth or harm, depending on how and where they are applied.

By recognizing this duality, you can approach feedback, relationships, and self-reflection with greater care, ensuring your actions create a more constructive, truthful, and compassionate world.
