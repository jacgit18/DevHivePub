## Crime Myth
The concept of "Black-on-Black crime" is a myth; the reality is that it’s poverty-driven crime. Crime is often a result of socioeconomic conditions, and the poor—who are disproportionately minorities, including Black people, due to systemic factors like racism and the legacy of slavery—are more likely to commit crimes out of desperation.  
  
When people are in poverty, they often victimize those within their immediate vicinity, which tends to be other poor individuals of the same demographic. Sometimes, they may venture outside their community into other areas to commit crimes, but the root issue remains economic inequality, not race.

## Nerd vs geek  
  
A nerd possesses knowledge about something fascinating but often lacks the practical skills or context to apply it in a way that benefits society.  
  
In contrast, a geek not only knows interesting things but also has the skills to apply that knowledge effectively. For example, a geek might have expertise in programming and use it to create useful tools, solve problems, or contribute meaningfully to technology and innovation.
