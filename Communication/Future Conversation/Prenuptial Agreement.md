1. **No Alimony:**  
- Specify in the prenup that neither party will be entitled to alimony in the event of divorce.  
  
2. **Each Party Covers Their Attorney Fees:**  
- Ensure that both parties are responsible for their own attorney fees related to the prenup process.  
  
3. **Regular Updates:**  
- Keep the prenup updated periodically to reflect any changes in circumstances or assets.  
  
4. **Timing of Signing:**  
- Avoid signing the prenup too close to the wedding date to prevent claims of coercion or duress.  
  
5. **Ceremony Venue:**  
- Consider having the wedding ceremony in a neutral location, such as an Airbnb, to avoid any legal jurisdictional issues.  
  
6. **Consider Emotional States:**  
- Be mindful of emotional states during prenup discussions, as agreements made under distress can be challenged in court.  
  
7. **Financial Investment:**  
- Have the spouse who benefits from the prenup cover the upfront costs of the wedding, ensuring both parties are financially invested for the long term.  
  
8. **International Marriage:**  
- Getting married internationally can offer legal advantages and potentially avoid certain U.S. laws regarding divorce settlements.  
  
9. **Contracts for Assets:**  
- Create contracts for specific assets, such as rings, detailing ownership and potential deductions from divorce settlements.  
  
10. **Alternative Wedding Contributions:**  
- Consider a non-traditional wedding where guests contribute financially instead of giving gifts, ensuring a greater sense of investment and commitment from all parties involved.
