## Culturally Homeless
Many people are "culturally homeless" or victims of other people's cultures and [[Value |values]] . Essentially, they begin to adopt and value what others value, such as a life of leisure and excess. This shift can bleed into relationships, creating unrealistic expectations, especially regarding finances. People start comparing their own lives and relationships to these cultural ideals, often leading to dissatisfaction or misunderstandings in partnerships.  
  
A key point is that many individuals, particularly in Western cultures, are influenced by the dominant individualistic culture, which can lead to self-centeredness. For example, in some relationships, especially among women, there can be a tendency to focus on what they can receive from a partner rather than what they can contribute. This mindset can create an imbalance, where both partners have unrealistic expectations of each other.  
  

### Culture & Emotion  
  
Our culture shapes much of how we experience and express emotions. It starts with rituals and ceremonies—cultural practices that help define our identity and emotional responses. From these rituals, we develop our emotions, and how we react to them creates feelings. When these feelings linger for a longer period, they evolve into moods.  
  
Understanding emotions requires having an emotional vocabulary, which is something we begin developing even before birth. The initial stages of attachment and detachment with our mothers are foundational in building this vocabulary. From there, we develop an emotional "expense account"—a mental and emotional framework for managing our feelings—and an emotional "budget," which dictates how much emotional energy we allocate to different aspects of life.  
  
This emotional framework is heavily influenced by culture, as we are constantly exposed to emotional cues and messages that shape our reactions and perceptions. We're sold on emotions all the time, whether it's through advertising or societal pressures. Ultimately, emotions are not purely personal; they are shaped by the culture we live in.
