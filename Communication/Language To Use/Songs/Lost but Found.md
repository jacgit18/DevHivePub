Key Changes and Rationale:

  

· Title & Hook: Changed from "Lost" to "Found" to immediately signal the reversal of the theme. The central hook becomes "I'm found in this recovery" instead of "I'm lost in these memories."

· Imagery:

  · "Just a scar" becomes "A light" – shifting focus from a permanent wound to an internal strength.

  · "I cannot repair" becomes "I can now declare" – moving from powerlessness to empowerment.

  · "I pretend it isn't there" becomes "I see the strength that's growing there" – acknowledging the past but focusing on growth.

· Time Perspective:

  · "I'm trapped in yesterday" becomes "I'm freeing myself today" – breaking the chain to the past.

  · "Where the pain is all I know" becomes "Where hope is all I know" – a fundamental shift in worldview.

· Empowerment vs. Victimhood:

  · "I will never break away" becomes "I will finally break away" and then "I will always break away" – showing a progression of certainty and strength.

  · "Lost all my dignity" becomes "Found my humanity" – reclaiming self-worth and compassion.

· The Bridge: The most significant change. "I try to keep this pain inside, but I will never be alright" is a cycle of suppression and hopelessness. The new version, "I try to let this healing in, and I know I will be alright," is a cycle of acceptance and hope. The repetition becomes a positive affirmation instead of a desperate cry.

· Tone of Confusion: "Living behind my own illusion / inside my own confusion" becomes "Living beyond my old illusion / outside my own confusion" – implying that the state of confusion is in the past and has been overcome.

  

This version aims to honor the raw emotion of the original while offering a path forward, transforming a song of anguish into one of resilience and hope.
