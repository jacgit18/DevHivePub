## Room of Phrases
> [!attention] **Formula**  
Observational Statement | | Assumption  + Open-ended Question → Specific Question → Compliment -> Repeat  

^c47e4a

Focus on making statement reframing your questions to be statements. 


A statement can act as an entryway to a topic by combining an observation or assumption with a subtle question. For example: “You don’t seem like you’re from here—are you European?” This approach funnels the conversation toward a topic while prompting the other person to reflect on why you might have made that assumption.

This technique works because people are often intrigued by how others perceive them, especially when the assumption is neutral or positive. It blends curiosity with flattery, making the interaction engaging and personal.

Alternatively you can go directly to a question but prime it by asking permission to ask a wild question or use a bridging statement or phrasing a question as a statement


**Use these words
	- "***That*** smile you just gave me? It’s got me curious... What’s going on in that mind of yours?"
	- "***That*** joke you made just now was _so_ on point. I’m wondering if you’ve got more hidden up your sleeve."
	- "***That*** look you just gave me? I’m guessing there’s something mischievous behind it. Want to share?"
	- Shift Focus with ***"You"*** Statements: Instead of bringing the conversation back to yourself, use statements that keep the focus on the other person. For example, "You must have felt proud after achieving that."  
	- Limit use of ***"I"*** if the goal is to keep focus on the other person.
	- Instead of shutting things down with a NO, offer alternatives. For instance: *"**But** I’ll need to think about it before I decide."* or *"**But** let me get back to you on that."*
	- Use **"wild"** instead of **"naughty"** to keep the tension lighthearted.

## Room of Drama
Be ready to walk away when needed. Stability is the foundation of a relationship, but fluctuations are inevitable.

The cycle starts with stability, followed by disruption—maybe even some deception—then a dramatic, movie-like push-and-pull moment before returning to stability.

Early on, you should be the one making the effort to reconcile. But as the relationship deepens, they should start taking on more of that responsibility.

### Room of Sparks
- Talk about things that excite you and invite them to share their passions ask [[High Priority Questions]],  [[Med Priority Questions]], and [[Low Priority Questions]]
- Try phrasing these question as a command or statement.
- *"Tell me something you can talk about for hours—something you’re obsessed with."*

	- *"Baseball? My dad used to hit me with a bat... okay, I made that up, but I’m not a fan of baseball."*  
	- Tell them about your day make it interesting maybe encourage them to guess.

**Focus on the Future** – Give them something to look forward to (_“Next time, I’m expecting an even better story.”_)

### **Room of Oscillation**

**Turn Conversations into Games**

Make it playful, keep them guessing, and turn every exchange into a challenge. Be fun-accusatory—like you’re onto them. Build excitement and anticipation with just enough confusion to keep them curious, making them ask, _"What’s next?"_ Be eager, but make them work for it. Add a competitive edge while keeping it light and fun. Most importantly, create an environment where they feel comfortable playing along.
