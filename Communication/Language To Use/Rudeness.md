## Callous vs Rudeness
The terms "callous" and "rude" both describe behavior that can be perceived negatively, but they differ in their underlying characteristics:  
  
1. **Callous:**  
- **Definition:** Callousness refers to an unfeeling or emotionally insensitive attitude. It implies a lack of empathy or compassion, often manifesting as indifference to the feelings or suffering of others.  
- **Nature:** Callous behavior may involve a deliberate or indifferent disregard for the emotional impact of one's actions, displaying a cold and emotionally detached demeanor.  
  
2. **Rude:**  
- **Definition:** Rudeness is characterized by behavior that violates social norms or conventions of politeness. It involves actions, comments, or gestures that show a lack of consideration or respect for others.  
- **Nature:** Rude behavior may not necessarily stem from a lack of empathy; rather, it can result from thoughtlessness, ignorance of social norms, or a momentary lapse in courteous conduct.  
  
In essence, callousness is more about emotional indifference and a lack of empathy, while rudeness is associated with breaches of social etiquette or politeness without necessarily indicating a lack of empathy. Both terms, however, describe behavior that can be perceived as negative or undesirable in social interactions.
