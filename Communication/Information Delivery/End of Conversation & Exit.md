Be intentional about ending an interaction—both in person and via text—while the conversation is at its most engaging. Aim to leave a positive, lasting impression that creates anticipation and curiosity.

#### Key Principles:

- **End Positively**: Wrap up the interaction on a high note, ensuring the other person feels good about the experience.
- **Leave Them Wanting More**: Avoid giving away too much about future plans or commitments. Let them wonder what comes next.
- **Be Memorable**: People tend to remember the most engaging moments of a conversation (about 5 minutes’ worth) and any strong positive or negative emotional experiences. Make the positive moments count.
- **Avoid Over-Explaining**: Keep your departure brief and lighthearted to avoid dampening the mood.

#### Examples for In-Person Conversations:

1. _"This has been a lot of fun—I’ve got to run, but I’m glad we met."_
2. _"I’m loving this conversation, but I have to go. Let’s pick this up another time."_
3. _"I’m heading out, but this has been great! Catch you around."_

#### Examples for Text Conversations:

1. _"This was fun—I’ll catch up with you later!"_
2. _"I can already tell we’re going to have a great time… but I’ve got to run for now."_
3. _"Gotta go, but I’ve really enjoyed this. Talk soon!"_

#### Pro Tips:

- **Don’t Overcommit**: Avoid mentioning specific future plans unless absolutely necessary. A hint of mystery keeps the other person intrigued.
- **Balance Positive and Negative**: While people remember negative moments, use them sparingly and in a playful way (e.g., light teasing) to add depth and variety without leaving a bad impression.
- **Be Aware of the Overall Arc**: Ensure the sum total of the interaction is positive, even if you include moments of light tension or mystery.

By leaving at the peak, you make sure the interaction ends on a high note, and you remain a memorable and intriguing presence in their mind.
